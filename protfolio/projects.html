This project involved analyzing a Telco customer dataset to understand factors influencing churn. Initial exploration revealed imbalanced data, with 73% not churning. Data cleaning included handling missing values and converting categorical variables. Insights emerged from univariate and bivariate analysis, showing higher churn among month-to-month contract holders, those without online security or tech support, and non-senior citizens. Additionally, customers with electronic check payment methods were more likely to churn. The findings suggest opportunities for targeted interventions to reduce churn and improve customer retention. The project culminated in saving the processed dataset for further analysis or modeling.
